Living Laos

Next summer, I'll be traveling with a backpack to Laos, Vientiane for humanitarian aid. I've always had the desire to do humanitarian aid and I'm glad I found a way to combine two of the things I like, which are traveling and giving back to others!! We are going to help by restoring the actual infrastructures that are in a bad state.

This includes building a clinic for the elephants, renovating the nursery, reconstructing the school and creating a water canal for the chosen community to have tap water. And we're going to do so much more!

    • I don't have friends, I have family.

    This project might be the most ambitious so far, as I think I've never invested myself that much into something but I really think it's for a great cause. It started from something I really wanted to do: helping others who haven't necessarily had the same opportunities as I did. I'm really excited to be doing this project with my good friend Gabrielle. We haven't known each other for that long, but we surely have values and similarities that made us closer. We decided out of the blue to do this humanitarian trip together as we do carry a common desire, which is to better our society.

    This project wouldn't be possible without my family and my friends' support. I grew up from a family that value everything, from tangibles like grains of rice to intangibles like education and I think every single doing of my family members shaped my morals and values. I wouldn't be able to write on my own website without the incredible help of Clément and Romain. I met them this summer through other friends and it's crazy the time and effort that they both put into this work. So grazie mille!!!

    And lastly, I'd like to thank everyone who made a donation so far! I collected $240 to this day and even though it's a huge amount, there's still much to do so keep helping by sharing it!!
    I'm really appreciative of you all and I'll be thanking you guys with a surprise to come. This first blog post was to thank everyone who contributed so far and for the ones who eventually will. I strongly believe in supporting and elevating one another and that reflects a lot in my actions.
    Thanks again :)

    • "Hold your liquid for the cause"

    Our bottles and mugs are finally here!

    As most of you guys know already, Gabrielle and I have been working hard for the past month to come up with bottles and mugs that would help finance our humanitarian trip to Laos. And all this wouldn't have been possible without the gracious contribution of Gabrielle's godmother, Michelle Sangollo. She has a business in printing and engraving all kinds of products. She is the mastermind behind the finished product and what we can't be more proud of. Thanks Michelle for making this happen and for your donation to our trip.

    We didn't want any products for the sake of selling it. We wanted something useful, practical, and affordable that you would like to buy.
    We thought bottles and mugs would be most appropriate to sell, as everyone is carrying one nowadays. Being myself a coffee addict, I would have never thought of needing one 2 years ago. Ever.
    The thought process of marketing a product is surprisingly exciting! From the brainstorming process, to the logo making, to the choice of bottles and mugs, it really is a fascinating venture.
    And now, they're ready for you!

    The bottles will be yours for $12 and the mugs for $15.
    Every proceed will go to the cause; helping children and helping elephants in Laos!!
    If you want to buy one, contact me so we can arrange to meet.

    Thanks again for all your help.
    Contact me if you want more information about Michelle Sangollo and her business!

"To give is to be happy with"

This project is truly about helping, caring, and giving back to those who most need it. VESA broad has many destinations where you can immerse yourself with communities in need and develop a bond with them by bettering their lives. Helping others comes with a cost and I need to collect $4000 in order to be able to do so. Small or big, I will accept all the help that is offered to me and every donation will go towards helping children and elephants!

For more information on my project, please feel free to contact me or visit the VESA broad official website at http://www.vesabroad.com

I'll be financing my trip with different projects and activities that I'll post here and on various social medias.  So stay tuned!!

Last but not least, I’d like to thank all of you for your donations, as it is greatly appreciated.
